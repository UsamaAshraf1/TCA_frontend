Why Change Feels Different in the Middle East (And What to Do About It)

April 23, 2025

Let’s be honest—change management anywhere is hard. But in the Middle East, it comes with a unique set of dynamics that many global playbooks just don’t address. 

When I first started consulting in the region, I expected resistance, but not the kind that’s rooted in silence. Silence in the Middle East doesn’t mean agreement. It often means caution, hierarchy, or deferral. In Western cultures, people might challenge openly. Here, it’s often wrapped in politeness, respect for authority, or simply not wanting to speak out of turn. But make no mistake: if people aren't bought in, the change won’t stick.

Another challenge is pace versus preparedness. There’s no shortage of ambition here—Saudi Vision 2030, Dubai’s digital transformation, and AI adoption across sectors. Leaders are moving fast. But teams? Often, they haven’t been brought along for the ride. There's a gap between vision and readiness, and that’s where change either gains traction or completely derails.

Decision-making is also deeply relationship-based. You can’t drop in with a toolkit and expect influence. You earn it through trust, context, and presence. I’ve seen projects get delayed—not for lack of strategy—but because the right person wasn’t consulted early on. Navigating power structures, formal and informal, is just as important as the framework you’re using.

And then there's culture—rich, collective, and deeply values-driven. If you don’t take time to understand the cultural anchors (family, tradition, identity), you’ll miss the very heartbeat of the people you’re trying to engage. Change isn't just about systems; it's about belonging.

So what works?

Start with people. Always. Listen more than you speak. Localize your language and approach. And focus less on pushing change, and more on unlocking momentum from within. The Change Agency was built to embed—not impose. We partner from the inside, blending change strategy with cultural fluency and emotional intelligence.

Because here’s the truth: change in the Middle East isn’t impossible. It’s just deeply human. And when you respect that, it’s incredibly powerful.
